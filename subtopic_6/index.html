Metro Manila Traffic:

Why it's so bad

Top 3 Traffic Violations

3. Number coding violations (30,474 incidents)

This widespread system meant to lessen the traffic congestion (and arguably makes it worse) goes as follows: certain cars, depending on the last number on their plate number, will be prohibited from driving at all. Some argue that this prompts many people to buy multiple cars, thus, making the traffic worse.

2. Obstruction (73,082 incidents)

In recent events, some incidents of homicide are due to road rage. Shootings and hit-and-runs are caused by traffic incidents. This, along with traffic incidents happen because of traffic congestion, unfortunately costing lives.

1. Disregard of traffic signs (91,843 incidents)

This widespread common problem — this gripe that the common people face — has turned deadly in recent years.

Accident-causing acts

Illegal parking

Illegal parking is obstruction(since you either stay on prohibited property, block the driveway, or stall traffic) and is punishable by fines from Php 150 to 2,000 and in some cases towing.

Reckless Driving

The Department of Justice defines "reckless driving" as:

“

1) Not considering the width, traffic, grades, crossing, curvatures, visibility, and other conditions of the highway and the conditions of the atmosphere and weather;
2) Endagering the property or the safety or rights of any person;
3) Causing excessive or unreasonable damage to the highway. In short, "Everytime a person operates a motor vehicle in violation of traffic rules, at the risk of harming others and with less than the full attention, focus, and skill required of every driver, he is driving recklessly."

Reckless driving is a punishable offense under Republic Act 4136, or the Land Transportation and Traffic Code.

Common forms of reckless driving include:

  • Over-speeding
  • Drunk-driving
  • Distracted driving
  • Street Racing
  • Aggressive driving

Motorcycle lane policy

The MMDA enforces a motorcycle lane policy in major roadways and to segregate motorcyces from heaver and larger vehicles. However, other vehicles can use the lane as it is not fenced, which may defeat its purpose. The motorcycle lane is supposed to make major roadways more orderly and safer for both motorcycle riders and other motorists. Motorcycles not using the lane can face a Php 500 fine.

Illegal Counterflow

Counterflowing is one of the most dangerous infractions as it can easily result in a collision if oncoming traffic faces the violator. This can result in car damage, injury, or even death. If not, oncoming traffic is likely to halt or to swerve to avoid the violator, contributing further to traffic.
